About Man O' War Nigeria

Learn about our history, mission, and leadership

Our History

Man O' War Nigeria was established in 1983 as a paramilitary youth organization under the leadership of the Nigerian government. The organization was created to promote discipline, patriotism, and community service among Nigerian youth.

Since its inception, Man O' War has grown to become one of the largest youth organizations in Nigeria, with presence in all 36 states and the Federal Capital Territory. The organization has trained millions of young Nigerians in leadership, survival skills, and community development.

Over the years, Man O' War has played significant roles in national events, including peacekeeping during elections, environmental sanitation exercises, and disaster response operations. Our members have consistently demonstrated the values of service, discipline, and patriotism.

Our Mission

To develop disciplined, patriotic, and responsible citizens through character molding, leadership training, and community service.

Our Vision

To be the leading youth organization in Nigeria that produces well-rounded citizens who contribute positively to national development.

Our Core Values

Service

Dedication to serving our communities and nation with selflessness and commitment.

Discipline

Upholding the highest standards of conduct, orderliness and self-control.

Patriotism

Fostering love for our country and commitment to its development and prosperity.

Our Journey

1983

Man O' War Nigeria was officially established by the Federal Government as a uniformed voluntary youth organization.

1985

First National Training Camp held in Lagos with participants from across the country.

1991

Expansion to all states of the federation with the establishment of state commands.

1999

Participation in national environmental sanitation exercises and peacekeeping during elections.

2008

Silver Jubilee celebration marking 25 years of service to the nation.

2020

COVID-19 response initiative providing support to communities across Nigeria.

2025

3rd National Camping Exercise held in Forest school, Aluu, Port-hacourt.
